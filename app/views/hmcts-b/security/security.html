Report an incident or breach

  • Content owner: MoJ HQ
  • Last updated: 27 March 2016
  • Tab 1

  • Read the security incident reporting procedure doc.
  • Fill out the security incident report form xls (separate guidance doc is available).
  • Email the form to Operational Security if the incident relates to IT.

All staff must report security incidents. HM Courts & Tribunals Service, NOMS and other office staff please use procedures on your local intranet.

We define a security incident as any action or event that deviates from MoJ security policies and controls, resulting in either actual loss of the confidentiality, availability or integrity of information or damage/harm to people or other assets; or the potential for this to happen. This would include, for example, a laptop being stolen (actual loss), or a key cabinet being left unsecured – even though there is no evidence of loss of assets (potential loss). The presence of new or unexpected threats should also be reported.

Security incident report form

You should complete a security incident report form for all security incidents – including IT, information, building, personnel. This will help us to stop the incident happening again and remove any potential security hazards or weaknesses.

You must report some incidents immediately. These include:

  • losses of personal data – within the first hour report to your line manager and either via them or directly to the most senior local manager and a local member of the Senior Civil Service (SCS); follow the steps outlined in data loss quick guide, and using the incident reporting form, report to the data access and compliance unit
  • incidents affecting ministers or members of the judiciary all assaults or serious threats to staff, clients or other service users demonstrations or escapes from custody
  • any incident that is likely to cause media interest
